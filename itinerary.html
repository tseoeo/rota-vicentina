Practical Info

Weather

Entirely dry trip — 0–2% rain every single day. Three independent models (AccuWeather, Met Office, meteoblue) all agree: no rain through Apr 25. Days 1–6 (Apr 15–20): Sunny and warming. Day 1 starts breezy (N 20–28 km/h) then winds ease. Highs climb from 21°C (Day 1) to 25°C by Day 3, stabilising around 23–24°C through Day 6. Day 7 (Mon 21 Apr) ⭐ WARMEST DAY — 26°C coastal trail, up to 29°C inland. Sunny, SE breeze 15–22 km/h. Carry extra water, start early, sun-exposed cliff walking. Day 8 (Tue 22 Apr): Big cool-down to 20°C, mostly cloudy, light W/WNW winds — refreshing reprieve. Days 9–11 (Apr 23–25): Fair skies, NNW wind builds. Mostly sunny, 20°C. Day 9: wind 15–24 km/h. Day 10 (Thu 24 Apr) ⚠ windiest day — NNW 20–29 km/h "gusty breeze" on exposed Sagres→Salema cliffs. Day 11: finish in breezy NW 15–22 km/h sunshine into Lagos. UV 5–6 daily — SPF 30+.

Forecast as of 13 April 2026. Sources: AccuWeather, UK Met Office, meteoblue (cross-verified). Check IPMA.pt for latest updates.

Essentials to Carry

2L+ water, windproof layer, rain jacket, sunscreen SPF 30–50, hat, packed lunch on remote days. Quick-dry towel for beach dips.

Tides Matter

Critical on Days 4 (Seixe river), 5 (Amoreira), 7 (Bordeira beach), 10 (Furnas-Figueira). Check tide tables nightly: Sines station (Days 1–4), Sagres station (Days 8–11).

25 April — Dia da Liberdade

National holiday. Banks & small shops closed. Large supermarkets open (reduced hours). Restaurants open. Stock up on 24 April. Lagos may have a concert at Praça Gil Eanes.

Mobile Coverage

Generally OK with MEO/NOS. Expect 2–4 hr dead zones on remote stages. Download offline maps + GPX tracks. GPS works without signal.

Cash

Almograve and Carrapateira have NO ATMs. Some places don't take cards. Carry cash from larger towns.

Worst service gaps (carry extra water + food): Day 1: 12–14 km gap · Day 3: 14 km gap · Day 7: 14 km gap · Day 8: 13 km gap · Day 9: 12.6 km gap

19.4 km 5h 08m +160m / -156m Hard (deep sand)

Trail Highlights

  • Praia dos Buizinhos — Porto Covo's iconic postcard cove with viewpoint above
  • Ilha do Pessegueiro — 17th-c fortress ruins on island 250m offshore; mainland fort with free access
  • White storks on sea cliffs — the only place in the world where storks nest on marine cliffs. The cliff-top trail passes directly above the nesting rocks between Praia dos Aivados (~km 10) and Praia do Malhão (~km 13–14). The first nest of the entire colony is on an isolated sea stack just south of Praia do Malhão. Active nesting in April. Viewing point →
  • Freshwater springs at Nascedios — groundwater emerges through the dune system
  • Mira Estuary panorama (~km 17) — first view of VN Milfontes. One of the trail's best moments
  • Forte de São Clemente — 1602 fortress at river mouth

Beaches along the way: Buizinhos, Engardaceira, Sissal, Ilha do Pessegueiro, Aivados, Saltinho, Nascedios, Malhão, Franquia (calm river beach at end).

Day 9 Wed 23 April

Vila do Bispo → Sagres

20.9 km 5h 41m +224m / -285m Hard
⛅ 20°C / 14°C Mostly sunny, wind building · 1% rain · Wind NNW 15–24 km/h · UV 5 · ↑06:47 ↓20:14
Service gap: After Vila do Bispo, ZERO water/food/shade for ~12.6 km to the São Vicente area. Flat farmland then exposed cliffs. Carry 2L+ water.

Trail Highlights

  • Torre de Aspa — highest sea cliff on Algarve coast (157m). Ruined watchtower. Views N to Cordoama, S to São Vicente

  • Forte do Beliche — 16th-c clifftop fortress, dramatic setting
  • Fortaleza de Sagres — Prince Henry the Navigator's fortress. Rosa dos Ventos (43m wind compass). ~3€ entry
  • Red-billed Chough — rare crow species on the cliffs
  • Endemic flora found nowhere else: Cistus palhinhae, Biscutella vicentina

Day 11 Fri 25 April — Dia da Liberdade

Praia da Salema → Lagos

22.9 km 6h 23m +430m / -430m Hard (long, most elevation)
⛅ 20°C / 13°C Intervals of clouds and sunshine · 0% rain · Wind NW 15–22 km/h · UV 5 · ↑06:45 ↓20:16 · Finish in breezy sunshine
National holiday: 25 April is Freedom Day. Banks & some shops closed, but restaurants, cafes, tourist sites open. Lagos may have celebrations at Praça Gil Eanes.
Service gaps: Salema to Burgau ~7 km (no water/food/shade). Burgau to Luz ~5 km (exposed clifftops). Carry water between stops.

Trail Highlights

  • Dinosaur footprints at Salema — 125-million-year-old Lower Cretaceous trackways at the start. Best at low tide
  • Roman ruins at Boca do Rio — mosaics, bathhouses, fish-salting factory. National heritage
  • Rocha Negra (Black Rock) — 70-million-year-old volcanic basalt cliff. Steep climb. Panoramic viewpoint at top — views to Sagres and Lagos. One of best on trail
  • Ponta da Piedade — crown jewel of Lagos coastline. Extravagantly eroded golden limestone sea stacks, arches, grottoes. 182-step staircase. Wooden boardwalk
  • Praia do Camilo — postcard-perfect tiny cove
  • Praia Dona Ana — famous Algarve beach, sheltered by golden cliffs
  • Spring orchids: Mirror Orchid, Bee Orchid between Salema and Burgau

Must-See Sights

  • Ponta da Piedade — golden sea stacks & grottoes. Boat tours ~20–30€. ~30 min walk south. Free, open 24/7
  • Igreja de Santo António — extraordinary gilded Baroque interior. One of the finest in the Algarve. ~3–4€ via museum
  • Slave Market Museum — Europe's first slave market (1444). Sobering, well-curated. ~3€
  • Old Town Walls & Governors' Castle — Manueline window where King Sebastião addressed troops (1578)
